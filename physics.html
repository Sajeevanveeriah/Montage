Relativity and Cosmology

Einstein Field Equations

Relate the geometry of spacetime (left side) to the energy-momentum content (right side).

Time Dilation

A moving clock runs slower. At v → c, the time dilation factor diverges.

Schwarzschild Radius

The radius within which an object's escape velocity exceeds c — the event horizon of a non-rotating black hole.

Hawking Temperature

Black holes emit thermal radiation. Larger black holes are colder.

Black Hole Entropy (Bekenstein–Hawking)

Entropy of a black hole is proportional to its horizon area A, not its volume.

Hubble's Law

Galaxies recede at a speed proportional to their distance. H₀ ≈ 70 km/s/Mpc.

First Friedmann Equation

Governs the expansion rate ȧ/a of the universe. k = curvature, Λ = cosmological constant.

Fluid Mechanics

Navier–Stokes Equation

Describes the motion of viscous fluid. One of the Millennium Prize Problems — global smooth solutions unproven.

Reynolds Number

Ratio of inertial to viscous forces. Re < 2300: laminar; Re > 4000: turbulent.

Newton's Law of Viscosity

Shear stress τ is proportional to the velocity gradient perpendicular to flow.

Coandă Effect

The tendency of a fluid jet to adhere to a convex surface. Normal pressure gradient provides centripetal force.

Bernoulli's Equation

Energy conservation along a streamline in inviscid flow: pressure + kinetic + potential energy per unit volume is constant.

Nuclear and Particle Physics

Mass–Energy Equivalence

A small mass corresponds to an enormous amount of energy; the basis for both nuclear power and weapons.

Radioactive Decay

Half-life: — the time for half the nuclei to decay.

Fission vs Fusion

  • Fission: Heavy nucleus splits (e.g. U-235 → Kr + Ba + neutrons). Used in current nuclear reactors.
  • Fusion: Light nuclei combine (e.g. D + T → He-4 + n). Powers the Sun; promising for clean energy.

Gamow Factor

Quantum tunnelling probability through the Coulomb barrier in nuclear fusion reactions. η is the Sommerfeld parameter.

Planck Time

The smallest meaningful unit of time; below this scale current physics breaks down.

10 Mind-Blowing Physics Discoveries

  1. Quantum entanglement — instantaneous correlation at any distance
  2. Dark energy — 68% of the universe, causing accelerating expansion
  3. Dark matter — 27% of the universe, detected only by gravity
  4. The Higgs boson — confirmed at CERN in 2012
  5. Gravitational waves — spacetime ripples detected by LIGO, 2015
  6. The double-slit experiment — particles behave as waves until observed
  7. Time dilation — verified with atomic clocks on aeroplanes
  8. The Big Bang — the universe is ~13.8 billion years old
  9. Hawking radiation — black holes slowly evaporate
  10. Neutron stars — a teaspoon weighs ~10 million tonnes
