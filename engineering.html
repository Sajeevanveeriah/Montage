NPN vs PNP Transistors

NPN: Current sinks into collector; base-emitter junction forward biased with positive voltage. Most common.

PNP: Current sources from emitter; requires negative base-emitter voltage. Used as high-side switches.

Sziklai (Complementary Darlington): NPN + PNP combined for high-current amplification with lower saturation voltage than standard Darlington.

Zener Diodes: Operate in reverse breakdown at a precise voltage — used for voltage regulation and protection.

Power Systems

kVA vs kW

kW (real power) does useful work. kVA (apparent power) is what flows through cables and determines heating losses, regardless of power factor. Equipment is rated in kVA because thermal limits depend on current, not just useful power.

Three-Phase Power

Three-phase systems deliver more power per conductor than single-phase. PF = power factor = cos(φ).

Transformer Components

Core (laminated silicon steel), primary winding, secondary winding, insulation, cooling (oil/air), tap changer, Buchholz relay (fault protection), conservator tank. Turns ratio: V₁/V₂ = N₁/N₂ = I₂/I₁.

Breaker Sizing Rule

Circuit breakers must be sized at ≥125% of Full-Load Amps (FLA) for continuous loads per NEC/IEC standards, ensuring the breaker does not trip under normal sustained load.

Grounding vs Earthing

Grounding: Connecting electrical circuits to a common reference point (0 V) for normal circuit operation.

Earthing: Connecting conductive parts to the physical earth to protect people from electric shock during faults.

UPS Types and Battery Backup

Offline/Standby: Switches to battery on mains failure (few ms delay). Line-interactive: Uses AVR to handle voltage fluctuations. Online double-conversion: Always running on inverter — zero transfer time, full isolation.

Semiconductors

From Sand to 3 nm: Chip Manufacturing Process

  1. Czochralski process: Silicon crystals grown from molten silicon
  2. Wafer slicing: Crystal ingot cut into thin wafers and polished
  3. Photolithography: UV/EUV light exposes photoresist through mask patterns
  4. Etching: Plasma removes exposed material to create features
  5. Ion implantation (Doping): Boron/phosphorus ions modify conductivity
  6. CMP (Chemical Mechanical Planarisation): Polishes surface flat after each layer
  7. Thin-film deposition: Oxide, nitride layers grown by CVD/ALD
  8. Metallisation: Copper interconnects deposited by electroplating
  9. Testing and packaging: Wafer probing, dicing, and chip packaging

Sensors and Transducers

Hall Effect

A current I in a conductor of thickness d, in magnetic field B, produces a transverse voltage VH. Used for current sensing, position detection, and speed measurement.

Piezoelectric Effect

Polarisation P produced by stress T, where d is the piezoelectric coefficient. Quartz and ceramics (PZT) produce voltage under mechanical stress. The inverse effect — strain under applied field — is used in ultrasonic transducers, inkjet nozzles, and precision actuators.

Gear Types

  • Spur gears: Teeth parallel to shaft axis. Simple and cheap, but noisy at high speeds.
  • Helical gears: Teeth at an angle. Smoother and quieter, but generate axial thrust.
  • Bevel gears: Conical shape for 90° direction change. Used in differentials.
  • Worm gears: High reduction ratios, self-locking. Used in lifts and winches.
  • Planetary gears: Compact, high torque density. Used in automatic gearboxes.

How a Steam Engine Works

Combustion heats water → steam pressure builds → steam enters cylinder → pushes piston → piston drives crankshaft → crankshaft rotates flywheel → flywheel smooths rotational output. Exhaust steam is either released or condensed and recycled.

Analogue Watch as Compass

Northern Hemisphere: Point the hour hand at the sun. South is halfway between the hour hand and 12 o'clock.

Southern Hemisphere: Point the 12 o'clock mark at the sun. North is halfway between the hour hand and 12.

Failure Modes in Concrete Beams

  • Flexural failure: Tension steel yields then concrete crushes
  • Shear failure: Diagonal cracking near supports
  • Compression failure: Sudden crushing — brittle, avoid by under-reinforcing
  • Bond/anchorage failure: Rebar pulls out
  • Deflection: Excessive long-term creep under service loads

Industrial Automation

Industrial Control Systems

  • PLC (Programmable Logic Controller): Rugged digital computer for machine control; runs ladder logic
  • SCADA (Supervisory Control and Data Acquisition): Remote monitoring and control of distributed plant
  • HMI (Human-Machine Interface): Touchscreen panels for operator interaction
  • DCS (Distributed Control System): Process control across multiple local controllers
  • VFD (Variable Frequency Drive): Controls AC motor speed by varying supply frequency
  • Servo controllers: Closed-loop position/velocity control with feedback encoder
  • Stepper controllers: Open-loop precise stepping for positioning

IT/OT convergence and Industry 4.0 connect shop-floor systems to enterprise IT for real-time analytics and predictive maintenance.

Robot Mechanisms

  • Linear actuators: Convert rotation to linear motion (pneumatic, electric, hydraulic)
  • Ball screws: Low-friction screw-nut pairs for precise linear positioning
  • Harmonic drives: Zero-backlash, very high reduction ratio — used in robot joints
  • Planetary gearboxes: Compact, high torque, multiple reduction stages
  • Joint types: Revolute (rotational), prismatic (linear), spherical (ball-and-socket)

RPA vs IPA

RPA (Robotic Process Automation): Rule-based bots that mimic human actions in UIs — clicking, copying, pasting. Brittle if UIs change.

IPA (Intelligent Process Automation): RPA augmented with AI/ML — OCR, NLP, decision-making. Can handle unstructured inputs and adapt to variation.

Networking

Data Centre Rack Architecture

Three-tier network: Access layer (connects servers) → Aggregation layer (policy and redundancy) → Core layer (high-speed backbone). PDUs (Power Distribution Units) run vertically in racks. Hot/cold aisle containment: servers draw cold air from cold aisle, exhaust to hot aisle, preventing recirculation.

VLAN Configuration

VLANs logically segment a physical network. Example: VLAN 10 (Management), VLAN 20 (Users), VLAN 30 (Servers). Trunk ports carry multiple VLANs using 802.1Q tagging. Port security restricts which MAC addresses can connect to access ports.

NAT Types

  • Static NAT (1:1): One private IP ↔ one public IP. Used for servers needing fixed external addresses.
  • Dynamic NAT (pool): Private IPs map to a pool of public IPs. Many-to-many but limited by pool size.
  • PAT / NAT Overload: Many private IPs share one public IP, differentiated by source port. Most common in home routers.
