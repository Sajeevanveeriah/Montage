Jordan Normal Form

Every square matrix over ℂ is similar to a block-diagonal matrix with Jordan blocks having λ on the diagonal and 1 on the superdiagonal. Canonical form for understanding matrix structure.

The Hessian classifies critical points: positive definite → local minimum, negative definite → local maximum, indefinite → saddle point.

Rank-Nullity Theorem

For a linear map T: V → W, the dimension of the image plus the dimension of the kernel equals the dimension of the domain.
